The myth of the active Australian

For decades, Australia has sold the world a sun-soaked, high-energy lifestyle - where the outdoors is second nature and activewear is the uniform. Three iconic campaigns helped sell this image of Australia to the world.

But does the data actually back this up?

🏃

Happiness drivers revealed

Let’s compare Australia and Switzerland

Switzerland achieves high levels of physical activity compliance, while Australia struggles with inactivity. Yet both countries rank among the world's happiest - challenging our idea that higher activity means higher happiness.

Switzerland has nearly 3× the physical activity compliance of Australia. Yet both countries rank highly in global happiness - suggesting that while physical activity supports health, it is not the defining factor behind national wellbeing.

Australia is happy
but not because of its activity levels

The "active Australian" is one of history's most successful national branding exercises. The reality: three-quarters of adults don't meet basic physical activity guidelines. Yet Australia ranks #10 globally for happiness - pointing to a weak link between activity and wellbeing.

The lesson is not that exercise doesn't matter - it does. But when explaining why Australia ranks among the world's happiest nations, the answer lies in its institutions, its social fabric, and its economic prosperity. Not its beaches.
